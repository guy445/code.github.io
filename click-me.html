The Amul Movement: A Revolution in White

The story of Amul is a testament to the power of cooperation, vision, and perseverance. From humble beginnings to becoming a household name, this journey has transformed India's dairy landscape.

1946: The Birth of a Movement

Farmers in Kaira district united against exploitative trade practices, forming the Kaira District Cooperative Milk Producers' Union under Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel's guidance.

1949: Dr. Verghese Kurien Arrives

A young engineer reluctantly posted to Anand would go on to become the architect of India's White Revolution and transform the dairy landscape forever.

1955: Amul Brand is Born

The name derived from Sanskrit "Amulya" (priceless) becomes a symbol of quality, trust, and farmer prosperity across India.

1970: Operation Flood Begins

The world's largest dairy development program replicates the Amul model nationwide, making India the world's largest milk producer.

1994: GCMMF Formation

Gujarat Cooperative Milk Marketing Federation unites various district unions, strengthening the cooperative movement.

2022: ₹72,000 Cr Brand

Amul becomes a ₹72,000 crore brand, owned by 3.6 million farmer families, proving the enduring power of cooperation.
